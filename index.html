Framing SOP

Overview

The Framing SOP outlines the full lifecycle of a framing job from initial scheduling through final completion and closeout. This process ensures consistency, quality, safety, and accountability across all Builders FirstSource Raleigh Install Services framing operations.

All framing jobs must be managed through Appwright, which serves as the central system for scheduling, communication, documentation, and task tracking. Every Project Manager is responsible for maintaining accurate and up-to-date information within Appwright throughout the duration of the job.

The goal of this SOP is to:

  • Ensure all jobs are properly scheduled and staffed
  • Maintain consistent build quality across all projects
  • Enforce safety standards aligned with “Drive to Zero”
  • Provide clear communication between Install, Sales, Builders, and Subcontractors
  • Document all work, changes, and issues for accountability

This SOP should be followed on every framing job without exception. Any deviations, change orders, or issues must be documented and approved through the proper channels before work continues.

Framing Services

Important Notes

  • A BFS project manager must be present at every service from start to completion.
  • If the assigned BFS project manager cannot attend, contact the framing install department immediately so coverage can be arranged.
  • Homeowner services should not be scheduled directly by a BFS project manager.
  • There is no excuse for missing service attendance. The PM is included at least one week in advance and must plan accordingly.

Service Process

  1. The builder sends a service request to the BFS sales team. Sales forwards the request to framing install so the service can be created, assigned, and homeowner contact can begin.
  2. Framing install creates and assigns the service in Appwright, then schedules with the homeowner by email while copying: sales, the requesting builder, and the BFS project manager handling the service.
  3. If the service is marked red / ON HOLD in Appwright, it is not ready for attendance. If it is marked ACTIVE, attend on the listed date and time.
  4. Review the worklist for both the service date range and the exact service date/time. The listed Sub Start Date-H/O Service and Sub Completion Date-H/O Service indicate the required attendance window.
  5. To ensure framer payment, the framer must be assigned to both the sub start and sub completion service tasks. Assign directly in Appwright or contact framing install for support.
  6. Add clear service notes in each service Appwright and upload all needed documents/photos. Use ALL JOBS when reviewing job documents to ensure complete visibility.
  7. After completion, upload before/after photos and service notes (or send a message to sales and framing install) detailing adjustments made during the service.
  8. Sign off every required worklist item once service work is complete.
  9. If service cannot be completed, notify sales and framing install immediately with a full explanation so the builder and homeowner can be informed.

Please note: “Apex Service” in Appwright does not schedule framing services. The framing install department performs homeowner scheduling. Include the Apex Framing Install team on service communications.

Back Charge to Framing Install Department Policy

Below is the Builders FirstSource Raleigh Framing Market policy regarding back charges issued to the install department. If proper communication and documentation is not provided, BFS Framing Install Department reserves the right to deny the request for back charges.

  1. BUILDER Communication
    • Builder must notify the BFS Project Manager immediately upon discovery of any issue that may result in a back charge to BFS.
    • To the extent possible, builder must provide up to 48 hours from time of discovery for BFS to rectify or react to the allegation. BFS will make best effort to react/resolve within 24 hours.
    • If the issue absolutely cannot wait, BFS still needs to be notified immediately and given an opportunity to approve the alternate solution.
  2. BFS PROJECT MANAGER Responsibility
    • BFS Project Manager will assess the situation, document the situation (video and/or pictures), propose solution to builder for approval, initiate action plan with framer, and document final resolution (video and/or pictures) in Appwright.
  3. BUILDER Documentation
    • Courtesy email notification of impending back charges should be sent to BFS Sales Team within 24 hours of final determination of cause. Photos are strongly encouraged and required whenever BFS is unable to visit site.
    • Formal back charge document (invoice, work order, PO, etc.) should be submitted to BFS Sales Team within 60 days of work completed. This should include:
      • Final charge amount
      • Name of trade partner being paid
      • Description of work required by trade partner
      • Description of issue that caused the need for additional work
  4. BFS SALES TEAM Communication & Documentation
    • Forward all back charge email notifications immediately to Install Management and the respective Project Manager.
    • Review formal back charge document (invoice, work order, PO, etc.) upon receipt from builder to ensure criteria is met BEFORE passing to Install Sales for review. Request clarification from builder when necessary.
    • Send formal back charge document to Install Management in a timely manner (within 10 business days of receipt) for review and approval.

NOTE:
By requiring time to assess and fix issues, BFS Framing Install Department will continue to grow with our subcontractors providing a higher standard. If not afforded this opportunity, the builder eliminates a great teaching opportunity, and the same issues are likely to continue.

Shaft Wall Repair – Townhomes

Shaft Wall – Townhome Construction

Shaft walls are a critical fire-rated and structural component in townhome construction. Proper installation is required to meet building code, fire separation requirements, and structural integrity.

Key Components

  • Gypsum shaft liner panels
  • 2” C-Runners (top and bottom)
  • ASW clips for attachment
  • Acoustical sealant (as required)
  • Fire blocking (per local code)
  • Air space and insulation as specified

Installation Requirements

  • Ensure shaft liner panels are installed plumb and continuous
  • Secure panels properly into C-Runners
  • Install ASW clips at required spacing
  • Seal all joints and penetrations with approved sealant
  • Maintain required air gaps and fire blocking

Critical Inspection Points

  • Foundation connection is properly aligned and sealed
  • Floor-to-floor continuity is maintained
  • Roof junction is properly framed and sealed
  • No gaps or breaks in fire-rated assembly

Important Notes

  • Shaft walls must meet all local fire code requirements
  • Improper installation can result in failed inspections
  • All deficiencies must be documented in Appwright

Reference Detail

ASW Breakaway Clip Placement

  • ASW up to 23' height: Space clips max of 10" vertically between wood or steel framing and "H" studs
  • ASW up to 44' height: Upper 24' of wall → 10" o.c. spacing
    Lower portion → 5' o.c. spacing
  • ASW up to 66' height: Upper 24' → 10" o.c.
    Next 20' → 5' o.c.
    Remaining wall → 40" o.c.

1-Hour Typical Offset Roof Detail

  • 5/8" glass mat exterior sheathing
  • 5/8" Type X gypsum wallboard
  • 1" shaft liner panels
  • Insulation per plan
  • Minimum 3/4" air space required

2-Hour Typical Offset Wall Detail

    Shaft Wall Repair – Nails Through Shaft Liner

    This procedure outlines the proper method for repairing damage caused by nails or fasteners driven through shaft wall liner panels in townhome construction.

    Issue

    • Contractors have driven or inserted 16d nails into the 1" shaft liner panels
    • Creates penetrations that compromise fire rating and system integrity

    Repair Procedure

    1. Remove nails from the shaft liner panels
    2. Fill holes with approved fire caulk (3M Fire Barrier Sealant CP 25WB+ or equivalent)
    3. Apply fire caulking on both sides of the wall where accessible
    4. Tool caulk smooth using putty knife or appropriate tool

    Important Notes

    • These conditions DO NOT affect the 2-hour fire rating of the shaft wall system
    • Do NOT over-repair (patching not required)
    • If damage penetrates the panel → follow “Hole Repair” procedure instead
    • All questionable conditions should be documented in Appwright

    Inspection Guidance

    • Verify damage is surface-level only
    • Confirm no full penetration of liner panel
    • If unsure, escalate to Framing Install Department

    Shaft Wall Repair – Missing / Damaged H-Studs or C-Runner

    This procedure outlines repairs required when H-studs or C-runner tracks are missing, damaged, or improperly installed in a shaft wall system.

    Issue

    • Missing or damaged H-studs
    • Missing or damaged C-runner track
    • Gaps between framing members
    • Improper installation affecting fire-rated assembly

    Repair Procedure

    1. Fill damaged or missing area with approved fire caulk (3M Fire Barrier Sealant CP 25WB+ or equivalent)
    2. Cut a metal flat strap (minimum 4" wide x 18" long)
    3. Center strap over damaged or missing section
    4. Secure strap to H-stud or C-runner using 1/2" Tek self-drilling screws
    5. Apply fire caulk around perimeter of metal strap
    6. If applicable, duplicate repair on opposite side of wall

    Important Notes

    • All repairs must maintain fire-rated system integrity
    • Improper framing must be corrected immediately
    • All repairs must be documented in Appwright

    Inspection Guidance

    • Verify framing continuity is restored
    • Ensure strap is securely fastened
    • Confirm all penetrations are sealed
    • Escalate major structural concerns to Install Manager

    Shaft Wall Repair – Up to 6" Hole in Shaft Liner

    This procedure applies to larger penetrations (up to 6") through shaft liner panels. These repairs require both internal liner patching and external drywall patching to maintain fire rating.

    Issue

    • Penetration through shaft liner up to 6 inches
    • Compromises full fire-rated assembly if not properly repaired

    Repair Procedure

    1. Cut two pieces of 1" shaft liner slightly smaller than the penetration
    2. Insert shaft liner pieces into cavity to fill void
    3. Laminate liner pieces together using approved fire sealant (3M Fire Barrier Sealant CP 25WB+)
    4. Secure laminated liner using 1-1/2" Type G laminating screws
    5. Apply fire caulk around edges of shaft liner repair
    6. Install 5/8" Type X drywall patch over opening
    7. Secure drywall patch using 1-1/4" drywall screws (max 6" spacing)
    8. Apply fire caulk around full perimeter of drywall patch
    9. If wall is accessible from both sides, duplicate repair on opposite side

    Important Notes

    • Both liner AND drywall patch are required for proper fire rating
    • All materials must meet ASTM / UL fire system requirements
    • Improper repair will result in failed inspection
    • All repairs must be documented in Appwright

    Inspection Guidance

    • Verify liner cavity is fully filled
    • Confirm drywall patch is secured properly
    • Ensure all edges are sealed with fire caulk
    • Check both sides of wall if accessible

    Window/Door Install Details

    Window Installation Standards

    • Verify flashing sequence
    • Ensure proper fastening pattern
    • Ensure caulk is not installed on frames
    • Verify there is no glass or frame damage
    • Document any damage seen with photos and a message in Appwright

    Door Installation Standards

    • Shim at proper locations
    • Maintain consistent reveals
    • Confirm sill pan installation
    • Ensure threshold is sealed properly
    • Quality of sill supports are good (no knots, wood damage, secured tightly)

    Common Issues

    • Daylight corrections
    • Binding doors
    • Out-of-square openings
    Window/Door Best Install Methods

    Window Best Practices

    1. Flashing Sequence (MOST IMPORTANT)
      • Always follow bottom → sides → top
      • Install sill pan/flashing first, set window into opening, side flashing, then head flashing last
      • Do not caulk the bottom nail fin of the window
      • Never reverse this order (causes water intrusion)
    2. Check Opening Before Install
      • Verify width & height, square (diagonal check), and level sill
      • If opening is off → STOP and notify framing
    3. Set & Secure Window Properly
      • Center window in opening
      • Tack top corners first
      • Recheck level, plumb, and square
      • Then fasten fully per manufacturer
    4. Shim Correctly
      • Shim at bottom corners, midpoints, and top if needed
      • Do NOT over-shim (causes bowing)
    5. Operation Check (ALWAYS)
      • Open / close / lock every unit
      • Check smooth movement, no binding, and locks engage

    Door Install – Best Practices

    1. Start with the Hinge Side
      • Set hinge side perfectly plumb first
      • This controls the entire door alignment
    2. Proper Shimming (CRITICAL)
      • Shim behind hinges, at strike plate area, and top corners
      • Never stack door pads, shim randomly, or force the door into place
    3. Check Reveals
      • Maintain even gap at top, hinge side, and latch side
      • Uneven reveal means frame is not square
    4. Threshold & Seal
      • Ensure threshold is fully supported and sealed tight to floor
      • No daylight; incorrect setup creates water intrusion risk
    5. Door Operation Check
      • Open/close smoothly
      • Latch easily and deadbolt aligns
      • If you have to force it, it’s wrong

    • Not checking RO before install

    Trim Install Details

    Casing Standards

    • Consistent reveal
    • Tight joints
    • Standard reveal: ~3/16 in. (builder dependent)
    • Must be consistent on all sides, straight/even, and with tight miter joints (no gaps)

    Failure Signs: Tight at top and wide at bottom, or wavy reveal.

    Joint Quality

    • Miters must be tight, flush, and clean

    DO NOT: Leave gaps or rely on caulk to fix bad cuts.

    Baseboard Standards

    • Tight to floor and wall
    • Proper inside/outside corners
    1. Floor Contact (most common issue): Base must sit tight to floor. If a gap exists, use scribing; do not leave visible gaps.
    2. Wall Contact: Base tight to wall with no bowing (wavy appearance = failure).
    3. Inside Corners (best practice): Use coping for tighter long-term fit.
    4. Outside Corners: Tight miters with no separation.
    5. Transitions/Terminations: Clean transitions at doorways and open walls.

    Crown Standards

    • Tight to ceiling
    • Clean joints
    Trim Install Best Practices

    Interior Door Install

    1. Hinge side first (most important rule): Set hinge side perfectly plumb and fasten it before anything else.
    2. Proper shimming: Place shims behind each hinge, at strike plate, and top of frame; shim and fasten through jamb into framing.
    3. Reveal standard: Keep ~1/8 in. consistent reveal at top, hinge side, and latch side.
    4. Latch/strike alignment: Latch should engage smoothly without force; adjust strike plate for minor correction.
    5. Final operation check: Door opens smoothly, closes without force, latches easily, and has no bounce-back.

    DO NOT: Shim randomly, skip hinge locations, or force frame into opening.

    Door Casing – Best Methods

    • Consistent reveal (standard ~3/16 in. from jamb), straight/even/consistent
    • Tight miters at corners with no visible gaps
    • Head casing level, side casing plumb, flush to wall

    DO NOT: Use caulk to fix poor joints.

    Baseboard Install – Best Methods

    • Tight to floor (scribe when floor is uneven; no visible gaps)
    • Inside corners: coping method
    • Outside corners: tight miters with no separation
    • Overall runs should look straight and smooth, not wavy

    Crown Molding – Best Methods

    • Tight fit to ceiling and wall (gaps are immediate failure)
    • Inside corners: coping preferred; outside corners: tight miters
    • Consistent angle and alignment with no dips/waves

    Finish Quality – What Separates Good from Great

    • Nail holes properly set and ready for fill
    • Caulking clean/minimal and used for finishing, not fixing
    • No dents/damage and clean lines throughout

    Common Install Failures

    • Doors: Hinge side not plumb, uneven reveals, door rubbing
    • Casing: Corner gaps, crooked install, caulk hiding poor cuts
    • Baseboard: Floor gaps, poor corners, wavy runs
    • Crown: Ceiling gaps, bad joints, misalignment

    Project Manager Field Check

    • Before install: Is opening correct? Is material good?
    • During install: Are they shimming correctly? Are joints tight?
    • After install: Would a homeowner notice anything wrong? Does everything look clean and consistent?
    Trim QC Checklist

    1) Pre-Install Site & Material Readiness

    Site Conditions

    • ☐ Drywall complete and finished
    • ☐ Floors installed (or sequence confirmed with builder)
    • ☐ House clean and ready for finish work
    • ☐ HVAC running for material acclimation

    Material Check

    • ☐ Correct trim package delivered (profiles, sizes)
    • ☐ No damaged, warped, or twisted material
    • ☐ Sufficient quantities onsite

    STOP RULE: Do not allow install with damaged or incorrect material.

    2) During Install (PM Site Visit Checks)

    Door Install Verification

    • ☐ Hinge side plumb
    • ☐ Frame square and secure
    • ☐ Door opens/closes smoothly
    • ☐ Latch engages without force
    • ☐ No rubbing or binding

    Casing Install

    • ☐ Consistent reveal (~3/16 in.) on all sides
    • ☐ Head casing level
    • ☐ Side casing plumb
    • ☐ Tight miters (no gaps)

    Look for uneven reveals and open corners.

    Baseboard Install

    • ☐ Tight to floor (no visible gaps)
    • ☐ Tight to wall (no bowing)
    • ☐ Inside corners coped
    • ☐ Outside corners tight

    Look for wavy runs and floor gaps.

    Crown Molding

    • ☐ Tight to ceiling and wall
    • ☐ Straight and consistent alignment
    • ☐ Clean joints

    Look for ceiling gaps and misaligned runs.

    Stair Trim (if applicable)

    • ☐ Handrails secure (no movement)
    • ☐ Balusters evenly spaced
    • ☐ Skirt boards tight to wall

    3) Safety Check (Every Visit)

    • ☐ Proper ladder use (no standing on top step)
    • ☐ PPE worn (eye protection, etc.)
    • ☐ Nail gun handled safely
    • ☐ Work area clean (no trip hazards)
    • ☐ Material stacked properly

    Document any violations in Appwright.

    4) Finish Quality (Most Important)

    Visual Inspection (standing height)

    • ☐ No visible gaps at joints
    • ☐ No crooked trim
    • ☐ No obvious defects

    Nail & Surface Check

    • ☐ Nail holes properly set
    • ☐ No blowouts or splits
    • ☐ No damaged pieces installed

    Caulking Standard

    • ☐ Minimal and clean
    • ☐ Not used to hide poor install

    If you can see the problem, it is not acceptable.

    5) Documentation (Appwright)

    • ☐ Casing corners photographed
    • ☐ Baseboard corners and runs photographed
    • ☐ Crown corners photographed
    • ☐ Any deficiencies documented
    • ☐ Notes entered clearly

    6) Deficiency Management

    • ☐ All issues marked clearly on site
    • ☐ All issues documented in Appwright
    • ☐ Subcontractor scheduled for correction

    DO NOT: Ignore small issues or assume they will be fixed later.

    7) Final PM Check (Before Sign-Off)

    • ☐ Entire house walked (no spot checks)
    • ☐ All trim installed per scope
    • ☐ No visible defects
    • ☐ Work ready for builder/homeowner

    Final Decision: PASS → Proceed to closeout. FAIL → Document and reschedule subcontractor.

    Purpose: Ensure all work is performed safely, protecting team members, subcontractors, and jobsite personnel while aligning with BFS “Drive to Zero” initiative.

    Standard: Safety is a non-negotiable requirement. All unsafe conditions must be corrected immediately and documented in Appwright.

    DRIVE TO ZERO

    All team members are expected to support BFS’s commitment to zero incidents by proactively identifying hazards, correcting unsafe conditions, and holding all team members accountable.

    Project Manager Responsibilities:

    1. Daily Safety Awareness
      • Perform visual safety checks on every jobsite that you visit
      • Identify hazards including:
      • Fall risks (open windows, stair openings)
      • Improper ladder usage
      • Debris and housekeeping issues
      • Unsafe tool usage
    2. Subcontractor Compliance
      • Ensure subcontractors are:
      • Wearing proper PPE (hard hats, gloves, eye protection as required)
      • Using ladders and equipment properly
      • Following safe install practices
      • Address unsafe behavior immediately
    3. Window & Door Specific Safety Risks
      • Large unit handling (team lift required when necessary)
      • Glass breakage hazards
      • Working from ladders during install
      • Improperly secured units during install process
    4. Safety Inspections (Appwright Task)
      • Minimum of 10 safety inspections are required each month.
      • Minimum of 30 safety inspections are required each quarter.
      • Each inspection must include:
      • Photos
      • Notes
      • Corrective actions, if required
      • For each section to be fully completed with photos
      • PM and subcontractor must acknowledge findings
    5. Corrective Actions
      • Immediate correction required for all safety violations
      • Document violation in Appwright
      • Repeat violations must be escalated to Safety Manager
    6. Communication & Escalation
      • Notify Safety Manager of serious violations immediately
      • Communicate issues to subcontractor leadership if needed
      • Document all conversations in Appwright

    IMPORTANT:

    • No job is more important than safety
    • Unsafe work must be stopped immediately
    • Failure to follow safety standards may result in removal from jobsite
