Framing SOP

Overview

The Framing SOP outlines the full lifecycle of a framing job from initial scheduling through final completion and closeout. This process ensures consistency, quality, safety, and accountability across all Builders FirstSource Raleigh Install Services framing operations.

All framing jobs must be managed through Appwright, which serves as the central system for scheduling, communication, documentation, and task tracking. Every Project Manager is responsible for maintaining accurate and up-to-date information within Appwright throughout the duration of the job.

The goal of this SOP is to:

  • Ensure all jobs are properly scheduled and staffed
  • Maintain consistent build quality across all projects
  • Enforce safety standards aligned with “Drive to Zero”
  • Provide clear communication between Install, Sales, Builders, and Subcontractors
  • Document all work, changes, and issues for accountability

This SOP should be followed on every framing job without exception. Any deviations, change orders, or issues must be documented and approved through the proper channels before work continues.

Appwright
1) Job Pre-Check – Framing (Appwright Task)

The purpose of this task is to start the scheduling process and check jobsite readiness. All job information (subcontractor, address, plans, truss documentation, etc.) is located in the Appwright worklist under the document section.

Site Assessment

  • Verify site ready within 2 days
  • Site graded and ready for deliveries
  • Site backfilled and safe for framers
  • If unsafe → contact builder for resolution
  • Unfilled porches must be blocked off
  • Temp power available (or request resolution)
  • Document if generator is needed
  • Foundation ready within 2 days
  • Measure pony walls / knee walls and send to Sales via Appwright
  • Ensure permitted plans are in permit box
  • Measure and order steel if applicable

Delivery Coordination

  • Basement / First Floor framing
  • Wall panels
  • Second floor framing
  • Second floor wall panels
  • Trusses

IMPORTANT: Send ALL schedule requests in Appwright. DO NOT call Sales or Framing for schedule changes.

2) Subcontractor Readiness

The Project Manager must verify subcontractor is assigned and ready. Do NOT rely on Appwright automated scheduling.

  • Confirm subcontractor availability directly
  • Install department schedules subcontractors (NOT PM)
  • Refer to Appwright worklist for assignments

If subcontractor is assigned:

  • Contact subcontractor to confirm dates
  • Confirm crew being used is approved
  • Confirm lumber delivery schedule

If subcontractor is TBD:

  • Contact Install Manager for assignment
  • Once assigned → follow normal process
  • Ensure plans are available for framer reference
3) Pre-Construction Meeting – Framing (Appwright Task)

The purpose of this task is to review and redline plans with the builder to ensure all selections are correct before framing begins.

Redline plans MUST be uploaded in Appwright for every job.

Plan Preparation

  • Ensure plan is available in permit box
  • If missing → coordinate with builder or framing department

Review Plans & Selections

  • Review with builder representative onsite
  • Mark all deviations or changes
  • Minor changes included at no cost if marked early
  • Major changes may require change order

Verification

  • Verify window & door rough openings
  • Confirm stair order and delivery date
  • Confirm truss delivery schedule

Documentation

  • Upload redline plans to Appwright
  • Label as “Redline Plans”
  • Ensure info is ready before handing to framer

Subcontractor Coordination

  • Schedule meeting with framing subcontractor
  • Review scope and redlines
  • Review foundation before start
  • Document any issues with photos
  • PM responsible for builder communication before start
4) Sub Start Date – Framing (Appwright Task)

SUB START DATE TASK TO BE ADJUSTED BY INSTALL MANAGEMENT ONLY

If you need the start date adjusted due to delays, send an Appwright to LIST: Install Framing for assistance. All other tasks are directly related to the sub start date task and proper care must be taken to ensure continuity.

  1. Verify material delivery prior to this date with Sales team.
  2. Sign off on task list in Appwright once framer has started
    • Date of sign off is important for tracking purposes and progression of project.

(1) Block Foundation

  • Open Floor inspection required before floor framing can be covered.
  • This is a courtesy inspection by most municipalities but required by BFS unless written approval is provided by builder.
  • Project Manager must verify readiness and coordinate repairs before inspection.
  • Coordinate scheduling with Builder:
    • Typically scheduled the day after framing starts
    • PM must confirm readiness before builder calls inspection
  • Once inspection passes, framing subcontractor can proceed.

(2) Basement Foundation

  • May require Open Floor inspection depending on municipality.
  • Contact builder representative for direction.
  • Inspection must pass before framing proceeds if required.
5) Project Manager Responsibilities During Construction
  1. Attendance at every jobsite is required daily.
  2. Appwright Scheduling Adjustments
    • Adjustments to the Task Schedule in Appwright are the sole responsibility of the Framing Project Manager.
      • Some items such as window and door delivery and install are directly linked to these tasks and it is imperative that the schedule is reviewed and adjusted regularly if needed.
  3. Job Safety
    • Job safety walks must be done at all times and daily for each building. Safety protocol can be found in Section IV of this handbook.
      • Verbal warnings must be given for all infractions. Documentation in Appwright is required.
      • If verbal warnings are not sufficient and infractions continue, contact the Safety Manager and Framing Install Department for further action.
  4. Communication
    • Daily conversation with framing subcontractor regarding quality, cleanliness, delivery, and job issues.
    • Communication with Builder regarding job issues and schedule for completion.
    • Communication with Sales and Framing Install Department via Appwright for materials, deliveries, and extra work.
    • Communicate delays due to subcontractor not being onsite.
  5. Material ordering must be communicated through Appwright.
  6. This process is important for tracking extra material for jobs for correction of takeoffs.
    • ALL MATERIAL REQUESTS NEED TO BE ENTERED IN APPWRIGHT WITH A REASON “WHY” THE MATERIAL IS NEEDED AND “WHAT” THE MATERIAL WILL BE USED FOR.
  7. (6) Order Stairs – verify timing and dimensions with Sales team
    • Contact Sales in Appwright for direction
    • Sales will typically need:
      • Total Rise from floor to floor
      • Dimension from framing of wall to framing
    8. 6) Safety Inspection Reports – Framing (Appwright Task)

    One safety inspection is required per job. 3 inspections per week, 12 per month, and 36 per quarter. This is a job requirement and must be completed to be compliant with OSHA regulations.

    1. Fill in each section including pictures, explanations, and corrective action plan if needed. All information will be uploaded from the jobsite.
      • Each field MUST have notes and photos.
    2. P.M. and Subcontractor representative must sign at the bottom of the report.
